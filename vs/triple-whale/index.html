02 — Head-to-head

How Bratrax Lite compares to Triple Whale.

These dimensions matter once you admit whether you truly need Triple Whale’s sprawling OS, or just decisive Shopify attribution.

Pricing: Flat vs. scales with your revenue

Bratrax Lite

$99/mo flat. Shopify GMV never rescales analytics cost. The invoice matches the marketed subscription.

Triple Whale

Tiered pricing scales with GMV. At $5M GMV, the public pricing page lands at $1,129/mo on the recommended plan.

Bottom line: At $5M GMV, the gap is $99/mo vs $1,129/mo — an ~11x difference. Triple Whale’s pricing follows the same logic as most enterprise SaaS: as you grow, you pay more. Bratrax skipped the sales team and the CS org that pricing pays for. That’s the only reason flat is possible.

Scope: Attribution layer vs. full intelligence platform

Bratrax Lite

Focused Shopify D2C attribution with all five core models. Dashboards tuned for $500K–$50M brands. TikTok and Klaviyo on the roadmap.

Triple Whale

Moby Chat, Moby Agents, MMM, incrementality testing, forecasting, Sonar (audience activation), creative analytics, and an optional managed data warehouse. Built for brands that want everything attribution-adjacent in one platform.

Bottom line: A broader platform is only valuable if you use the breadth. For a $3M Shopify brand with one or two people running ads, most of that surface goes unused — and you pay for the full platform regardless. For a $15M brand with a marketing team that needs creative analytics, forecasting, and activation in one workflow, the case for one platform is real.

AI: Your model vs. their Moby

Bratrax Lite

Connect your own Claude or ChatGPT to your Bratrax data using your own API key. You pay your AI bill directly. No vendor markup, no proprietary chatbot to trust.

Triple Whale

Moby Chat and Moby Agents are Triple Whale's proprietary AI products, built on top of their data model. Their positioning is explicit: “AI that actually works for ecommerce — this isn't ChatGPT with your data bolted on.”

Bottom line: Whose AI is interpreting your business — Triple Whale’s, or yours? Moby reads your data through Triple Whale’s data model, on Triple Whale’s assumptions. With Bratrax, you bring Claude or ChatGPT and ask your own questions of the same data. Same facts underneath. Different judgment layer on top.

Onboarding: Focused tool vs. broader platform setup

Bratrax Lite

Connect Shopify, Meta, and Google. Dashboards open immediately. The narrow scope means there’s not much to set up.

Triple Whale

Self-serve onboarding via the Triple Pixel. Their site says “most brands are up and running with Triple Whale in 15 minutes, with no engineer required.” Higher tiers add a dedicated Implementation Specialist and CSM.

Bottom line: The real contrast here isn’t fast setup vs. slow setup. It’s narrow tool vs. broad platform. A focused attribution product has fewer pieces to configure by design. Triple Whale’s setup is light for an enterprise suite, but you’re still configuring a multi-product suite.

Data portability

Bratrax Lite

Exports are part of the product — not a paid add-on. You own your data, not just access to a dashboard.

Triple Whale

A managed data warehouse is available at higher tiers. Standard plans don't clearly document what data export looks like, so ask Triple Whale directly before signing if portability matters to your evaluation.

Bottom line: The dashboard isn’t the real product. The dataset behind it is. Every report, filter, and breakdown is constrained by how the vendor modeled your data before you logged in. If your attribution history lives inside their platform, switching later means rebuilding more than dashboards — it means rebuilding the data layer underneath. Bratrax keeps the dataset portable, so your analytics doesn’t depend on renting one vendor’s view of your business.

Service & support: self-serve vs. analyst-supported

Bratrax Lite

Your team runs Bratrax Lite themselves. Documentation-led setup. No analyst, no CSM.

Triple Whale

Higher tiers include an Implementation Specialist and a dedicated CSM. Built for teams that want hands-on account support.

Bottom line: Bratrax Lite is built for teams that want to run attribution themselves. If your team needs implementation support and a dedicated CSM, that’s a real need — but a different conversation, and a different Bratrax product than Lite.

03 — Receipts

Quotes worth checking before you pick Triple Whale.

“Other tools tell you what happened. Triple Whale tells you what to do next — with unified data, proven measurement, instant activation, and AI that knows your business as well as you do.”
Triple Whale homepage

This is the claim worth questioning. “Tells you what to do next” sounds stronger than reporting. It implies judgment. But the right next move is not determined by attribution data alone. It depends on margin, inventory, cash flow, creative fatigue, offer strategy, team capacity, and what risk the business is willing to take. Triple Whale may give useful recommendations inside its model, but that is not the same as knowing your business as well as you do. The cleaner question is: do you want a vendor’s AI interpreting your business for you, or your own AI working on portable data you already trust?

“All prices are based on a combination of your brand’s annual revenue and the package you choose.”
Triple Whale pricing page

Triple Whale’s own pricing page states the model plainly. Your bill grows as your revenue grows. At $5M GMV, the public pricing page shows $1,129/mo on the recommended plan. That is not a promotional number or a high-tier add-on; it is the standard paid tier for a brand at that scale.

“The app is okay, but it's full of bugs and the UI is terrible… They're clearly gearing it toward larger enterprises… for a small operation it's just way overload.”
Shopify App Store review

That review is useful because it speaks directly to fit, not just frustration. G2’s aggregate for Triple Whale is strong at 4.5/5 from 479 reviews, while Trustpilot is lower at 3.0/5 from 38 reviews. The point is not that Triple Whale is a bad product; it is that smaller operations can feel the platform is overbuilt for what they need. Lindsey Sullivan’s Trustpilot review says the other side clearly: “It’s pricey but worth it.” That is the profile Triple Whale is built for.

04 — Who Bratrax Lite is for

Who should choose Bratrax Lite.

  • You run a Shopify D2C brand in roughly the $500K-$50M range. The strongest fit is often $1M-$20M revenue with a lean team that needs clear attribution without enterprise overhead.
  • You want predictable analytics cost structure. Flat $99/mo pricing works best when you do not want analytics cost to rise with GMV tiers as the business scales.
  • You prefer focused attribution over all-in-one platform breadth. If you do not need MMM, forecasting, activation, and broader intelligence modules, Bratrax Lite keeps scope narrow.
  • You want portable data plus BYO AI workflows. If your team already uses Claude or ChatGPT and wants direct access to attribution data for custom analysis, Bratrax Lite is built for that workflow.
05 — When Triple Whale is the right choice

Choose Triple Whale if this sounds like your team.

  • You want a feature-complete ecommerce intelligence platform. Triple Whale publicly positions as replacing 10+ tools (attribution, creative analytics, forecasting, activation, MMM, and more under one roof). If you want breadth and consolidation, Bratrax Lite is deliberately narrower.
  • You need MMM, incrementality testing, or forecasting. Bratrax Lite does not have these. Triple Whale’s platform includes them. For brands at a scale where those methods are genuinely useful, Triple Whale’s price is easier to justify.
  • You want a built-in AI agent ecosystem, not BYO AI. Moby Chat and Moby Agents are purpose-built for ecommerce workflows. If you want pre-trained ecommerce AI instead of bringing your own general-purpose model, Triple Whale’s approach makes more sense.
  • You are spending $200K+/month on ads and need the full platform depth. The GMV-scaled pricing is harder to justify at $1M–$5M revenue. At $10M–$30M, the analytics complexity, team size, and measurement needs are more likely to match what Triple Whale has built.
  • You want a dedicated Customer Success Manager and implementation support. Higher Triple Whale tiers include structured onboarding and account management. Bratrax Lite is self-serve: there is no CSM included.

06 — How to switch

Switching from Triple Whale in three steps.

Step 1 — Connect Bratrax Lite alongside Triple Whale. Connectors take ~10 minutes (Shopify, Meta, Google Ads). Both tools run in parallel.

Step 2 — Compare attribution side by side. Run for a week or two. Verify Bratrax matches or improves on what Triple Whale has been showing you.

Step 3 — Cut over. Cancel Triple Whale when you’re confident. If you’re on a managed warehouse tier, export anything you want to keep before canceling.

What about your history? Bratrax pulls your Shopify order history and runs attribution on past orders. From the day you connect Bratrax, you have attribution drawn from what Shopify already captured — and from that point forward, every new order gets full Bratrax precision, with every paid-channel click captured directly by our pixel.

If it’s not the right fit: 30-day money-back guarantee. No annual lock-in. The risk of trying is one month of $99.

07 — FAQ

Bratrax Lite vs Triple Whale FAQs.

What is Bratrax Lite?

Bratrax Lite is a flat $99/mo Shopify D2C attribution tool with self-serve setup, portable data, and the ability to bring your own Claude or ChatGPT through a standard API key. Built by the team behind Inceptly ($950M+ in D2C revenue driven) and VidTao (100K+ marketers).

What is Triple Whale?

Triple Whale is a GMV-priced ecommerce intelligence platform that consolidates attribution, AI agents (Moby Chat / Moby Agents), creative analytics, MMM, forecasting, and audience activation under one roof. It positions as "replacing 10+ tools" for ecommerce brands.

How is Bratrax Lite different from Triple Whale?

Bratrax Lite is a self-serve, flat-priced Shopify D2C attribution product. Triple Whale is a full ecommerce intelligence platform covering attribution, AI agents, creative analytics, forecasting, activation, and more, with revenue-tiered pricing. Different scope, different pricing model, different buyer.

Is Bratrax Lite cheaper because it does less?

Yes, honestly. Bratrax Lite does not have Moby Agents, MMM, incrementality testing, forecasting, creative analytics, or Sonar activation. It does five attribution models well for Shopify D2C brands running Facebook and Google. Triple Whale’s feature surface is genuinely broader. The question is whether you need that breadth and whether the pricing gap at your GMV is justified by how much of it you use.

Does Bratrax Lite have a free plan like Triple Whale?

No free plan. Bratrax Lite starts at $99/mo flat. Triple Whale’s free plan exists but is limited: for a brand running paid ads across multiple channels, the paid tier is where attribution features live.

Does Bratrax Lite have something like Triple Whale’s Moby?

Not a proprietary AI agent. Bratrax Lite lets you connect your own Claude or ChatGPT with your own API key. You bring the AI; Bratrax provides the data. Triple Whale’s Moby is a deeper, ecommerce-specific AI product: more integrated, more opinionated, and tied to the Triple Whale platform.

What attribution models does Bratrax Lite include?

All five: first-touch, last-touch, linear, time-decay, and position-based. All included, no tier unlock required. Triple Whale has 7+ models including AI-powered options like Total Impact.

Can I try Bratrax Lite before committing?

Yes: 30-day money-back guarantee, no annual contract, cancel anytime.
